Converting Bankruptcy Chapters in Tennessee

If your financial situation changes after filing, Tennessee debtors can convert between bankruptcy chapters. Understanding your conversion rights can help you get the best outcome.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Conversion Options

Chapter 7 to Chapter 13

  • One-time right of conversion under section 706(a)
  • Common when you have assets at risk of liquidation
  • Useful when income increases or you want to save property
  • Must be able to propose a feasible repayment plan
  • No additional filing fee

Chapter 13 to Chapter 7

  • Right to convert at any time under section 1307(a)
  • Not available if case was originally Chapter 7 and converted to 13
  • Common when income drops and plan payments become unaffordable
  • Must still pass the means test if required
  • No additional filing fee

Common Reasons for Conversion in Tennessee

  • Job loss or income reduction - Chapter 13 plan payments become unaffordable, convert to Chapter 7
  • Income increase - No longer pass the means test for Chapter 7, convert to Chapter 13
  • Property at risk - Chapter 7 trustee identifies non-exempt property, convert to Chapter 13 to protect it
  • Mortgage arrears - Need Chapter 13's structured repayment to catch up on missed mortgage payments and keep your home (homestead exemption: $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint))
  • Plan failure - Unable to complete Chapter 13 plan, convert to Chapter 7 for immediate discharge

What Happens When You Convert

  1. File a motion or notice of conversion with the W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn. bankruptcy court
  2. New schedules may be required - particularly if significant time has passed or circumstances have changed
  3. New trustee is assigned - Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 trustees are different
  4. The automatic stay continues - no gap in protection
  5. A new 341 meeting may be scheduled - the new trustee needs to examine you

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert my bankruptcy case to a different chapter?

Yes. Under 11 U.S.C. section 706 and section 1307, you generally have the right to convert between chapters. Chapter 7 debtors can convert to Chapter 13 once as a matter of right. Chapter 13 debtors can convert to Chapter 7 at any time if they have not previously converted from Chapter 7.

Does converting restart my case?

Converting does not start a completely new case. Your case number stays the same, and the automatic stay remains in effect. However, new schedules and forms may need to be filed, and certain deadlines reset.

Are there fees to convert?

There is no additional filing fee to convert from Chapter 13 to Chapter 7. Converting from Chapter 7 to Chapter 13 requires paying the difference in filing fees ($313 minus $338, so no additional fee). Attorney fees may apply for the additional work.
