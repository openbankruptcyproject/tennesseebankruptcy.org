Chapter 13 Payments in Tennessee

Your Chapter 13 plan payment is based on your income, expenses, and debts. Here is how it works in Tennessee and what to do if you cannot keep up.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

How Payments Are Calculated

Three tests determine your minimum Chapter 13 payment:

1. Disposable Income Test

Your monthly income minus reasonable living expenses (IRS standards and actual secured debt payments). All disposable income must go into the plan.

2. Liquidation Test (Best Interest of Creditors)

Unsecured creditors must receive at least what they would get if you filed Chapter 7. If you have significant non-exempt assets, this raises your payment.

3. Secured and Priority Debt Test

Your plan must pay all priority debts in full (taxes, child support arrears) and cure any secured debt arrears (mortgage, car loan). These are non-negotiable minimums.

Plan Length in Tennessee

Below Median Income

36-60 months. Your plan can be as short as 3 years but cannot exceed 5 years. Shorter plans mean higher monthly payments but less time in bankruptcy.

Above Median Income

60 months (required). If your income exceeds the Tennessee median for your household size, you must commit to a full 5-year plan.

What Gets Paid in a Chapter 13 Plan

Debt TypeTreatment
Mortgage arrearsPaid in full to cure default and keep home (homestead exemption: $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint))
Car loanPaid through plan, possible cramdown if older than 910 days
Priority taxesPaid in full over plan length
Child support arrearsPaid in full
Unsecured debtPaid 0-100% depending on disposable income
Trustee feesTypically 5-10% of plan payments, built into payment amount

Missing Payments and Modification

If you fall behind on Chapter 13 payments:

  • Contact the trustee immediately - many will work with you on temporary hardship
  • File a plan modification if the change is permanent (job loss, pay cut, medical issues)
  • Hardship discharge may be available under section 1328(b) if you have paid the equivalent of what Chapter 7 creditors would have received
  • Convert to Chapter 7 if you no longer have sufficient income for any plan
Do not simply stop paying. Communicate with the trustee and your attorney to explore all options before your case is dismissed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will my Chapter 13 payment be in Tennessee?

Your monthly payment depends on your disposable income (income minus allowed expenses), the amount of secured and priority debts you must pay, and the liquidation test (unsecured creditors must receive at least what they would get in Chapter 7). Most Tennessee Chapter 13 payments range from a few hundred to a few thousand dollars per month.

What happens if I miss a Chapter 13 payment?

Missing payments can result in the trustee filing a motion to dismiss your case. If your case is dismissed, you lose the protection of the automatic stay and all the progress you have made. Contact the trustee immediately if you are going to be late - many allow temporary payment modifications.

Can I change my Chapter 13 payment amount?

Yes. You can file a modified plan with the W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn. court if your financial situation changes. Common reasons include job loss, pay cut, medical emergency, or increased expenses. The modified plan must still meet all legal requirements.
