Can I Travel During Chapter 13 in Tennessee?

Domestic travel is fine. International travel may require notifying your trustee. The real issue is not where you go - it is what you spend.

Quick answer: Domestic travel during Chapter 13 in Tennessee is unrestricted. For international travel, notify your trustee as a precaution. The key rule: do not spend money on luxury travel while your creditors are being paid less than they are owed.

Domestic vs. International Travel

Domestic Travel

  • No restrictions or permissions needed
  • No notification to trustee required
  • Keep spending reasonable
  • Budget travel is generally fine
  • Work travel is never an issue

International Travel

  • No legal prohibition, but notify your trustee
  • Some trustees want advance notice
  • Extended trips may raise questions
  • Keep records of purpose and expenses
  • Luxury international travel is risky

What Could Cause Problems

  • Falling behind on plan payments: If you travel and then miss a plan payment, the trustee will connect the dots. This is the fastest way to get your case dismissed.
  • Lavish spending: Posting photos from expensive resorts while paying creditors 10 cents on the dollar creates a credibility problem. Trustees and creditors can review social media.
  • Unexplained income: If you are traveling frequently on a budget that says you have no discretionary income, expect questions about undisclosed income or assets.
  • Extended international trips: Spending months abroad can suggest you have income or assets not disclosed in your schedules.
The safest approach: keep travel modest, stay current on plan payments, and notify your trustee before any international travel.

What Is Always Fine

  • Work travel: Required by your employer, fully reimbursed - no issue whatsoever
  • Family emergencies: Travel for funerals, medical emergencies, or family crises
  • Local/regional day trips: Taking the kids to a state park or visiting family
  • Budget vacations: A modest road trip or camping trip within your means
  • Medical travel: Traveling for medical treatment or appointments

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I travel during Chapter 13 in Tennessee?

Yes. Domestic travel is generally not restricted during Chapter 13 in Tennessee or any other state. International travel may require notifying your trustee, depending on local practice. The key concern is whether travel spending conflicts with your budget and plan obligations.

Do I need court permission to travel during Chapter 13?

For domestic travel, no court permission is needed. For international travel, some trustees and courts want advance notice. There is no statute requiring permission, but your plan requires you to devote all disposable income to creditors - luxury travel could raise issues.

Can the trustee stop me from traveling?

The trustee cannot directly stop you from traveling. However, if you spend money on non-essential travel and then fall behind on plan payments, the trustee can file a motion to dismiss your case. Lavish travel spending can also trigger a motion to modify your plan upward.

Is international travel allowed during Chapter 13?

There is no law prohibiting international travel during Chapter 13. However, best practice is to notify your trustee before international trips. Some trustees view international travel as a sign that you have more disposable income than reported.

What about work travel during Chapter 13?

Business travel is not an issue. If your employer requires travel, that is a legitimate expense. Keep records showing the travel was work-related, especially if questioned by the trustee.

Can I take a vacation during Chapter 13?

Modest vacations are generally acceptable. The concern is extravagant spending while creditors are receiving less than what they are owed. A reasonable road trip or modest vacation is unlikely to cause problems. A cruise or international resort vacation may attract scrutiny.
