How to Stop Wage Garnishment in Tennessee

Bankruptcy stops most wage garnishments the same day you file. Here is how it works.

General educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Garnishment Limits in Tennessee

Tennessee follows federal garnishment limits (25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30x federal minimum wage, whichever is less), with a small additional exemption for dependents.

Protected income: Social Security, disability benefits, child support (received), veterans benefits, and most pensions are generally exempt from garnishment, though they may still be garnished for federal taxes or child support.

Some creditors ignore the rules or calculate the protected amount incorrectly. If you think your paycheck is over-garnished, request a hearing to object. Bankruptcy is often the faster, more permanent solution.

How Bankruptcy Stops Garnishment

The moment a bankruptcy petition is filed, the automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 takes effect. This federal court order stops:

  • All wage garnishments by non-governmental creditors
  • Bank account garnishments and attachment liens
  • Collection calls and letters
  • Pending lawsuits and judgments
  • Foreclosure and vehicle repossession proceedings

To stop the garnishment you must notify your employer's payroll department and the garnishing creditor the same day you file, so they halt withholdings on the next paycheck. Provide the case number and the filing date.

Recovering Already-Garnished Wages

If a creditor garnished more than $600 from you within the 90 days before you filed, those payments are preferential transfers under 11 U.S.C. section 547 and can be clawed back. The trustee will recover the funds from the creditor. In some cases (if you have unused exemption room) you can claim the recovery for yourself.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can be garnished from my paycheck in Tennessee?

Tennessee follows federal garnishment limits (25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30x federal minimum wage, whichever is less), with a small additional exemption for dependents.

Does bankruptcy stop garnishment immediately?

Yes. The automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 takes effect the exact moment your bankruptcy petition is filed. You must notify your employer's payroll department and the garnishing creditor the same day so they halt withholdings on your next check.

Can I recover wages that were already garnished?

If a creditor garnished more than $600 within the 90 days before you filed, you can often claw it back as a preferential transfer under 11 U.S.C. section 547. Involuntary payments within the 90-day look-back are recoverable by the trustee or (if you have exemption room) by you.
