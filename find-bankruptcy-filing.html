How to Find Bankruptcy Filings in Tennessee

Bankruptcy filings are public records. Here is how to search them in Tennessee -- from free options to the official PACER system.

Quick answer: Search PACER at pacer.uscourts.gov ($0.10/page, $3 cap per doc). For free access, use CourtListener.com (RECAP archive) or call the Eastern, Middle, and Western Districts of Tennessee clerk's office. The Tennessee bankruptcy court website is https://www.tnmb.uscourts.gov.

Option 1: PACER (Official System)

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) is the federal court system's official electronic records platform.

  • Website: pacer.uscourts.gov
  • Cost: $0.10 per page, capped at $3.00 per document. Quarterly charges under $30 are waived.
  • Search by: Name, case number, Social Security number (last 4), date range
  • Tennessee court: https://ecf.tnmb.uscourts.gov
  • Registration: Free to create an account. Takes about 5 minutes.

Option 2: RECAP / CourtListener (Free)

RECAP is a free browser extension that contributes PACER documents to a public archive. CourtListener hosts this archive and makes it searchable.

  • Website: courtlistener.com
  • Cost: Completely free
  • Coverage: Millions of documents, growing daily. Not every document is available, but many are.
  • Browser extension: Install RECAP for Chrome or Firefox. When you view PACER documents, RECAP automatically saves a copy to the public archive.

Option 3: Court Clerk's Office

You can contact the Eastern, Middle, and Western Districts of Tennessee bankruptcy court clerk's office directly:

  • Phone: Call the clerk and request a case search by name or case number. Basic case information (filing date, chapter, status) is typically provided for free.
  • In person: Visit the courthouse and use the public access terminals. No PACER account needed.
  • Website: https://www.tnmb.uscourts.gov may have a free case search or CM/ECF lookup

What Information Is Available

  • Docket sheet: Every document filed in the case, with dates and descriptions
  • Petition: Debtor's name, address, chapter filed, attorney
  • Schedules: Assets, liabilities, income, expenses (with sensitive info redacted)
  • Claims register: Every creditor claim filed in the case
  • Orders: Court orders, discharge, dismissal
  • Meeting of creditors (341): Date and outcome
Sensitive information is redacted: full Social Security numbers, complete financial account numbers, and the names of minor children are not visible in public filings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find bankruptcy filings in Tennessee?

Bankruptcy filings in Tennessee are public records available through PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) at pacer.uscourts.gov. You can search by name, case number, or Social Security number. The Eastern, Middle, and Western Districts of Tennessee has its electronic filing system at https://ecf.tnmb.uscourts.gov.

Is PACER free?

PACER charges $0.10 per page viewed, capped at $3.00 per document. Quarterly fees under $30 are waived. You can also use RECAP (a free browser extension) to access previously downloaded documents at no cost via CourtListener.com.

Can I search bankruptcy filings for free?

Yes. CourtListener.com (via the RECAP archive) has millions of free bankruptcy documents. Many court websites also offer free case information lookups. The bankruptcy court clerk's office will search records for free over the phone for basic information.

Which courts handle bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Bankruptcy cases in Tennessee are filed in the Eastern, Middle, and Western Districts of Tennessee. The court website is https://www.tnmb.uscourts.gov. PACER electronic filing is at https://ecf.tnmb.uscourts.gov.

How long are bankruptcy records available?

Bankruptcy records on PACER are generally available for the life of the case plus several years. Older cases may be archived but still accessible. Bankruptcy stays on your credit report for 7 years (Chapter 13) or 10 years (Chapter 7) from the filing date.

Can I see someone else's bankruptcy filing?

Yes. Bankruptcy filings are public records. Anyone can search PACER or visit the court clerk to view case documents. Some sensitive information (Social Security numbers, financial account numbers) is redacted from public filings.
