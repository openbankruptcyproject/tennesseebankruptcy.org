Emergency Bankruptcy Filing in Tennessee

When foreclosure, repossession, or garnishment is days or even hours away, Tennessee residents can file an emergency bankruptcy petition to trigger the automatic stay immediately.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Is an Emergency Bankruptcy Filing?

An emergency bankruptcy filing -- sometimes called a bare-bones or skeleton filing -- is when you file only the minimum required documents to open a case and activate the automatic stay. You file the rest within 14 days.

Minimum Documents Required

  • Voluntary Petition -- the two-page form that officially opens the case
  • Creditor Matrix -- a list of all known creditors with names and addresses
  • Credit Counseling Certificate -- proof of the required pre-filing credit counseling course (can be completed online in about an hour)

When to Consider Emergency Filing in Tennessee

Emergency Filing Makes Sense When

  • A foreclosure sale is scheduled within days
  • Your employer received a garnishment order that starts next pay period
  • A creditor is actively repossessing your vehicle
  • A bank account levy is about to clear
  • A lawsuit judgment is about to be enforced
  • Utility shutoff is imminent

Better to File a Complete Petition When

  • You have several weeks before any deadline
  • You want to avoid the risk of missing the 14-day supplement deadline
  • Your financial situation is complex and needs careful preparation
  • You need time to gather all financial documents

The 14-Day Deadline

After an emergency filing in W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn., you have 14 days to file all remaining documents:

  • Schedules A/B through J (assets, liabilities, income, expenses)
  • Statement of Financial Affairs
  • Means Test calculation (Form 122A or 122C)
  • Tax returns for the most recent year
  • Pay stubs for the 60 days prior to filing
If you miss the 14-day deadline and do not receive an extension, the court will dismiss your case. This can limit the automatic stay protection in any future filing within one year.

Tennessee Exemption Quick Reference

When you complete your schedules, you will need to claim exemptions to protect your property:

  • Homestead: $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint)
  • Vehicle: $10,000
  • Federal exemptions: Tennessee allows you to choose between state and federal exemption systems

For a complete list, see the Tennessee exemptions page.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file an emergency bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes. You can file an emergency (bare-bones) bankruptcy petition in Tennessee to get the automatic stay protection immediately. You file only the two-page voluntary petition, a creditor list, and proof of credit counseling. You then have 14 days to file the remaining schedules and documents.

How fast can the automatic stay take effect in Tennessee?

The automatic stay takes effect the instant your petition is filed with the W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn. bankruptcy court. If you file electronically during court hours, it can happen within minutes. Emergency filings are designed for situations where a foreclosure sale, wage garnishment, or repossession is imminent.

What happens if I miss the 14-day deadline?

If you fail to file the remaining schedules and documents within 14 days, the court may dismiss your case. You can request a one-time extension, but it is not guaranteed. A dismissal could limit your ability to refile and get the automatic stay again.
