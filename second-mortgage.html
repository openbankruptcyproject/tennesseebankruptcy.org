Second Mortgages and Bankruptcy in Tennessee

If your Tennessee home is worth less than your first mortgage, Chapter 13 lien stripping can eliminate your second mortgage entirely.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Is Lien Stripping?

Lien stripping is a powerful tool available in Chapter 13 bankruptcy under 11 U.S.C. section 506(a). It allows you to remove a junior mortgage (second mortgage, HELOC, or other junior lien) from your home when:

  1. Your home's current fair market value is less than the balance on your first mortgage
  2. The second mortgage is therefore wholly unsecured -- there is no equity supporting it
  3. You file a motion with the W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn. court asking to strip the lien

Once the Chapter 13 plan is completed, the second mortgage lien is permanently removed from your property.

How It Works

1

Get Home Appraised

Determine your home's fair market value. It must be less than the first mortgage balance for the second mortgage to qualify for stripping.

2

File Lien Strip Motion

Your attorney files a motion to value the collateral and strip the wholly unsecured junior lien. The second mortgage holder can object.

3

Complete the Plan

The second mortgage is treated as unsecured debt in your 3-5 year plan. Upon plan completion and discharge, the lien is permanently removed.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 for Second Mortgages

Chapter 7

Lien stripping is not available in Chapter 7 per Bank of America v. Caulkett (2015). The personal liability on the second mortgage can be discharged, but the lien survives. The creditor cannot collect from you personally, but the lien remains attached to the property.

Chapter 13

Lien stripping is available. If the home value is below the first mortgage balance, the second mortgage lien is stripped and the debt is treated as unsecured. Upon successful plan completion, the lien is permanently removed from your title.

Tennessee Homestead Exemption Impact

The Tennessee homestead exemption of $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint) protects your home equity from creditors. In a lien stripping scenario, the exemption is less relevant because the home is underwater -- but it becomes critical if property values recover.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I strip a second mortgage in bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes, through a process called lien stripping in Chapter 13. If your home is worth less than what you owe on your first mortgage, the second mortgage is considered wholly unsecured and can be treated as general unsecured debt in your plan. At completion of the plan, the lien is removed.

Can I strip a second mortgage in Chapter 7?

No. The Supreme Court ruled in Bank of America v. Caulkett (2015) that Chapter 7 debtors cannot strip wholly underwater junior liens. Lien stripping is only available in Chapter 13 (and Chapter 11) in Tennessee.

What if I have some equity above the first mortgage?

If your home value exceeds the first mortgage balance even slightly, the second mortgage is not wholly unsecured and cannot be stripped. The full second mortgage balance would need to be addressed in your Chapter 13 plan or reaffirmed.
