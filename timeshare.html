Timeshares and Bankruptcy in Tennessee

Trapped in a timeshare? Bankruptcy may be the most reliable exit. You can surrender the timeshare and discharge the mortgage, maintenance fees, and assessments.

Quick answer: You can surrender a timeshare in Chapter 7 bankruptcy and discharge all associated debts -- mortgage, maintenance fees, assessments, and special levies. This is often faster and cheaper than timeshare exit companies.

Chapter 7: The Clean Exit

Chapter 7 is the most straightforward way to exit a timeshare:

  • List the timeshare as property on Schedule A/B (real or personal property, depending on how the timeshare is structured)
  • Indicate surrender on your Statement of Intention (Schedule D/Form 108)
  • Discharge the debt: The mortgage balance, maintenance fees, assessments, and any other obligations are discharged
  • Deed transfer: The timeshare company typically handles transferring the deed out of your name after discharge
Important: You are responsible for maintenance fees that accrue between filing and the date the timeshare is actually transferred. Work with the trustee to expedite the surrender.

Chapter 13: Keep or Surrender

Chapter 13 gives you more options:

  • Surrender through plan: Include the timeshare surrender in your Chapter 13 plan. Pre-petition arrears are treated as unsecured debt, paid at the plan percentage (often 10-30 cents on the dollar).
  • Keep and pay: If you want to keep the timeshare, continue paying the mortgage and fees. Include any arrears in the plan.
  • Lien stripping: If the timeshare is worth less than what you owe, you may be able to strip the lien in some circumstances -- though this is more common with second mortgages on primary residences.

What Debts Are Covered?

  • Timeshare mortgage: Discharged in Chapter 7, paid through plan in Chapter 13
  • Maintenance fees (past due): Discharged as unsecured debt
  • Special assessments: Discharged as unsecured debt
  • Exchange program fees: Discharged (RCI, Interval International, etc.)
  • Collection agency debts: If the timeshare company sold the debt, the buyer's claim is also discharged

Bankruptcy vs. Timeshare Exit Companies

Bankruptcy

  • Filing fee: $338 (Chapter 7)
  • Legally binding discharge
  • Eliminates ALL timeshare debts
  • Process takes 3-4 months
  • Court-supervised, transparent
  • Also eliminates other qualifying debts

Exit Companies

  • Typical cost: $3,000-$10,000+
  • No legal guarantee of results
  • High rate of failure/scams
  • Process can take 12-24 months
  • No regulatory oversight
  • Only addresses the timeshare

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get rid of a timeshare through bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes. In Chapter 7, you can surrender a timeshare and discharge the remaining obligations -- mortgage balance, maintenance fees, and assessments. The timeshare interest is listed as property of the estate and abandoned or surrendered through the bankruptcy process.

Will bankruptcy stop timeshare maintenance fees?

Bankruptcy discharges your personal liability for pre-petition maintenance fees. However, maintenance fees that accrue after filing are your responsibility until the timeshare is actually transferred out of your name. Surrendering in bankruptcy accelerates that process.

What happens to a timeshare mortgage in bankruptcy?

A timeshare mortgage is treated like any other secured debt. In Chapter 7, you can surrender the timeshare and discharge the debt. In Chapter 13, you can either keep paying it or surrender it through the plan. The lender may still foreclose on the timeshare interest itself.

Can I keep my timeshare in Tennessee bankruptcy?

Yes. If you want to keep the timeshare, you can reaffirm the debt (Chapter 7) or continue paying through your plan (Chapter 13). You must stay current on mortgage payments, maintenance fees, and assessments.

Are timeshare exit companies legitimate?

Many timeshare exit companies charge thousands of dollars and deliver no results. Bankruptcy is often a more reliable and less expensive way to exit a timeshare. A Chapter 7 filing fee is $338 -- far less than most exit company fees.

Does the timeshare HOA get paid in bankruptcy?

Pre-petition HOA/maintenance fees are unsecured debt and typically receive little or nothing in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, they are paid at the same rate as other unsecured creditors -- often pennies on the dollar.
