Buying a House After Bankruptcy in Tennessee

Bankruptcy does not permanently prevent homeownership. Thousands of Americans buy homes after bankruptcy every year. Here are the waiting periods, requirements, and strategies for Tennessee residents.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Waiting Periods by Loan Type

Loan TypeAfter Chapter 7After Chapter 13
FHA Loan2 years from discharge1 year into plan (with court approval) or 2 years from discharge
VA Loan2 years from discharge1 year into plan (with lender approval)
USDA Loan3 years from discharge1 year into plan (with approval)
Conventional (Fannie/Freddie)4 years from discharge2 years from discharge or 4 years from dismissal
The waiting period starts from the discharge date, not the filing date. For Chapter 7, that is typically 3-4 months after filing. For Chapter 13, it is at the end of the 3-5 year plan.

Steps to Prepare for Homeownership

1

Rebuild Credit

Get a secured credit card immediately after discharge. Make small purchases and pay in full monthly. Consider a credit-builder loan. Target a 620+ score for FHA, 640+ for conventional.

2

Save for Down Payment

FHA requires 3.5% down with a 580+ score (10% with 500-579). Conventional requires 3-5%. VA and USDA offer zero-down options.

3

Stabilize Employment

Lenders want to see 2 years of stable employment. Consistent income documentation is critical. Self-employed borrowers may need additional tax returns.

Tennessee Homestead Exemption

When you do buy a home in Tennessee, the state homestead exemption protects $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint) in home equity from future creditors. This means:

  • Your home equity up to $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint) is protected from judgment creditors
  • If you ever need to file bankruptcy again, this equity is exempt from the Chapter 7 trustee
  • Tennessee allows choosing between state and federal exemptions, giving flexibility in protecting your home

Frequently Asked Questions

How long after bankruptcy can I buy a house in Tennessee?

After Chapter 7: 2-4 years depending on the loan type (FHA requires 2 years, conventional requires 4 years). After Chapter 13: 1-2 years after discharge (FHA may allow applications during an active plan with trustee approval). VA loans require 2 years after Chapter 7 and may allow applications during Chapter 13.

Can I get a mortgage during Chapter 13?

Some lenders and the FHA allow mortgage applications during an active Chapter 13 plan if you have made 12 consecutive on-time payments and receive court and trustee approval. This is difficult but not impossible.

Will bankruptcy destroy my credit score?

Bankruptcy causes an initial credit score drop of 100-200 points. However, many Tennessee filers see improvement within 12-18 months because the debt-to-income ratio improves and negative accounts stop accumulating late payments. Active rebuilding efforts accelerate recovery.
