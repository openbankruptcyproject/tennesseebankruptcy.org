Car Leases and Bankruptcy in Tennessee

You have two options: assume the lease (keep the car) or reject it (return the car). The 60-day clock starts when you file.

Quick answer: When you file bankruptcy in Tennessee, you must decide within 60 days whether to keep or surrender your leased vehicle. If you keep it, you must stay current. If you surrender it, remaining lease obligations become dischargeable unsecured debt.

Assume vs. Reject: Your Two Options

Assume (Keep the Car)

  • Continue making lease payments as normal
  • Cure any past-due payments
  • Lease continues as if bankruptcy never happened
  • Must not be a financial burden on your budget
  • Must decide within 60 days of filing
  • Good option if payments are affordable and you need the car

Reject (Return the Car)

  • Surrender the vehicle to the leasing company
  • Early termination fees become unsecured debt
  • Remaining lease payments become unsecured debt
  • Deficiency balance discharged in Chapter 7
  • Paid at reduced rate in Chapter 13 plan
  • Good option if payments are too high or car is not needed

The 60-Day Deadline

Under 11 U.S.C. section 365(d)(2), you have 60 days from the date of filing to assume or reject your car lease in Chapter 7. This deadline can be extended by the court for cause, but extensions are not automatic.

If you fail to act within 60 days, the lease is deemed rejected. This means you lose the vehicle and the remaining obligations become unsecured debt.

Mark your calendar: 60 days from your bankruptcy filing date is your deadline for the lease decision. Missing it means automatic rejection.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 Lease Treatment

Chapter 7

  • 60-day assume/reject deadline applies
  • Rejected lease: deficiency balance discharged
  • Assumed lease: continues normally
  • No option to modify lease terms
  • Best if you want a clean break from a bad lease

Chapter 13

  • Decision made as part of your plan
  • Can cure past-due payments through plan
  • Rejected lease: deficiency paid through plan at reduced rate
  • More flexibility on timing
  • Best if you are behind on payments but want to keep the car

Practical Considerations

  • Mileage and wear: If you are over your mileage limit or the car has excess wear, rejecting the lease in bankruptcy eliminates those penalties. They become dischargeable unsecured debt.
  • Gap insurance: If the car is worth less than the payoff, gap insurance may cover the difference. Bankruptcy rejection does not trigger gap insurance - it covers total loss/theft only.
  • New lease after bankruptcy: You can lease a new car after bankruptcy, but expect higher deposits and potentially higher rates. Some Tennessee dealers work with recent bankruptcy filers.
  • Automatic stay protection: While your case is pending, the leasing company cannot repossess the vehicle. If you plan to reject, you have time to arrange alternative transportation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to my car lease if I file bankruptcy in Tennessee?

In both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13, you must decide whether to assume (keep) or reject (surrender) your car lease within 60 days of filing. If you assume, you keep the car and continue making payments. If you reject, you return the vehicle and any remaining balance is treated as unsecured debt.

What does it mean to assume a lease in bankruptcy?

Assuming a lease means you agree to keep the lease, stay current on all payments, and cure any past-due amounts. The lease continues as if bankruptcy was never filed. You must be current on payments and the vehicle must not be a financial burden.

What does it mean to reject a lease in bankruptcy?

Rejecting a lease means surrendering the vehicle to the lessor. Any early termination fees, remaining payments, or deficiency balance become unsecured debt that can be discharged in Chapter 7 or paid through a Chapter 13 plan at pennies on the dollar.

What is the 60-day deadline for lease decisions?

Under 11 U.S.C. section 365(d)(2), you must assume or reject an unexpired lease within 60 days of filing (for Chapter 7). If you do not act within 60 days, the lease is deemed rejected. In Chapter 13, you can assume or reject as part of your plan.

Can I get out of a bad car lease through bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes. Rejecting a car lease in bankruptcy is one of the cleanest ways to exit a bad lease. Early termination fees and remaining balance become unsecured debt. In Chapter 7, this debt is typically discharged entirely.

Will the leasing company repossess my car during bankruptcy?

No. The automatic stay prevents the leasing company from repossessing the vehicle once you file. However, you must make your decision within 60 days and stay current on payments if you want to keep the car.
