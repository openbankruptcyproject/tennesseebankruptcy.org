Bankruptcy and Security Clearance in Tennessee

Filing bankruptcy does not automatically revoke your security clearance. In many cases, resolving debt through bankruptcy can actually help your clearance by eliminating financial vulnerability. Here is what clearance holders in Tennessee need to know.

Quick answer: Bankruptcy does NOT automatically revoke your security clearance. The adjudicative guidelines recognize that taking responsible steps to resolve debt -- including filing bankruptcy -- can be a mitigating factor. Unresolved, spiraling debt is a bigger clearance risk than filing bankruptcy to resolve it.

Guideline F: Financial Considerations

Security clearance adjudications are governed by the Adjudicative Guidelines, specifically Guideline F (Financial Considerations). The concern is that financial problems can indicate poor self-control, unreliability, or vulnerability to pressure.

However, Guideline F also lists mitigating conditions, including:

  • The behavior happened under unusual circumstances (job loss, medical emergency, divorce) and is unlikely to recur
  • The individual has initiated and is adhering to a good-faith effort to resolve debts -- bankruptcy qualifies
  • The conditions that resulted in the financial problem were largely beyond the person's control
  • There are clear indications the problem is being resolved or is under control

Filing bankruptcy directly addresses multiple mitigating conditions. It eliminates the debt, demonstrates proactive behavior, and removes the financial vulnerability that is the actual clearance concern.

Tennessee Military and Defense Presence

Tennessee hosts Fort Campbell (on the KY/TN border), Arnold Air Force Base, the Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and the Y-12 National Security Complex. Numerous defense and energy contractors operate in the state. Filing bankruptcy does not automatically revoke clearance. Oak Ridge in particular has a high concentration of clearance-required positions.

Self-Reporting Requirements

If you currently hold a security clearance, you are typically required to self-report significant financial events. This includes:

  • Filing for bankruptcy (either chapter)
  • Judgments or liens against you
  • Significant delinquencies (typically 120+ days past due)
  • Wage garnishments

Report through your Facility Security Officer (FSO) or Security Manager. Timely self-reporting demonstrates integrity and compliance -- two qualities adjudicators value highly.

Failing to report a bankruptcy filing when required is a separate security concern. The cover-up is worse than the event itself. Always report promptly.

Why Bankruptcy Can Help Your Clearance

Without Bankruptcy

  • Growing debt creates ongoing vulnerability
  • Debt collectors and creditors pursuing you
  • Potential garnishments and judgments
  • Financial stress affects job performance
  • Adjudicator sees unresolved problems

After Bankruptcy

  • Debt eliminated or structured into a plan
  • No more creditor pressure or coercion risk
  • Clean financial slate going forward
  • Demonstrates proactive problem-solving
  • Adjudicator sees responsible resolution

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I lose my security clearance if I file bankruptcy?

No. Bankruptcy does not automatically revoke clearance. It can demonstrate financial responsibility by resolving debt that creates vulnerability.

Should I tell my security officer?

Yes. Self-reporting is typically required and demonstrates integrity. Failure to report is a separate concern.

Can bankruptcy help my clearance?

Yes. Eliminating debt removes financial vulnerability -- the actual clearance concern. Adjudicators recognize bankruptcy as a responsible step.

Is Chapter 13 better than Chapter 7 for clearance?

Chapter 13 is sometimes viewed more favorably because it shows a repayment commitment. But Chapter 7 is also recognized as a legitimate debt resolution method.

Can I get a new clearance after bankruptcy?

Yes. Many people receive clearances after bankruptcy. Adjudicators evaluate the whole person, not just the bankruptcy filing.
