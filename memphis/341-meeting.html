The 341 Meeting in Memphis -- What to Expect

The 341 meeting of creditors is a brief hearing about 30 days after you file bankruptcy. Here is what Memphis filers need to know.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting of creditors (named after 11 U.S.C. section 341) is a required hearing where you answer questions under oath about your finances, assets, and the information in your petition.

The meeting is conducted by the Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 trustee, not a judge. It typically lasts 5-10 minutes if everything is in order.

Where and When

Location: 341 meetings in Memphis are typically held at or near the W.D. Tenn. courthouse at 200 Jefferson Ave, Suite 413, Memphis, TN 38103.

Timing: Usually scheduled 20-40 days after filing. You will receive a notice with the exact date, time, and location.

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Social Security card or proof of SSN
  • Recent pay stubs (last 60 days)
  • Most recent tax return
  • Bank statements (last 2-3 months)

Common Questions Asked

  • Did you review the petition before signing?
  • Is the information accurate?
  • Do you own any real estate?
  • Have you transferred or sold any property recently?
  • Are you expecting any inheritance or lawsuit settlement?
  • Do you have any additional bank accounts?
