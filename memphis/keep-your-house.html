Can You Keep Your House in Memphis Bankruptcy?

Tennessee has a low state homestead exemption, but you can choose federal exemptions -- which protect far more equity. Here is how to keep your home.

Homestead Exemption Comparison

SystemHomestead Protection
Tennessee state (individual)$5,000
Tennessee state (family/62+)$7,500
Federal$27,900
Federal exemptions protect over 5x more home equity than Tennessee state exemptions. Most Memphis homeowners should choose federal.

Non-Judicial Foreclosure Warning

Tennessee is a non-judicial foreclosure state. Your lender can foreclose without going to court, with as little as 20 days' notice. The bankruptcy automatic stay is the most effective way to stop a non-judicial foreclosure.

Chapter 7 vs Chapter 13 for Homeowners

Chapter 7

  • Keep home if equity is within exemptions
  • Must be current on mortgage or catch up quickly
  • Does not help with mortgage arrears

Chapter 13

  • Catch up on missed payments over 3-5 years
  • Strip off junior liens if underwater
  • But: 79.6% dismissal rate in W.D. Tenn.
