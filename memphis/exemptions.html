Memphis Bankruptcy Exemptions

Tennessee allows you to choose between state and federal exemptions - a significant advantage. Compare both systems to protect the most property.

State vs. Federal Exemptions

Tennessee is one of the states that lets filers choose between state exemptions and the federal bankruptcy exemptions under 11 U.S.C. section 522(d). You must pick one system - you cannot mix and match.

For many Memphis filers, federal exemptions are the better choice because the federal homestead exemption ($27,900) is much higher than Tennessee's ($5,000/$7,500).

When to Choose State Exemptions

  • You have little or no home equity but significant personal property
  • Tennessee's $10,000 wildcard covers your most valuable assets
  • You have large household goods collections (state: $4,000)

When to Choose Federal Exemptions

  • You own a home with equity between $7,500 and $27,900
  • You need the federal vehicle exemption ($4,450)
  • You are not married (state homestead is only $5,000 for individuals)
  • You want to stack the unused homestead as wildcard ($1,475 + up to $13,950)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can married couples double exemptions in Tennessee?

If both spouses file jointly, each spouse claims their own set of exemptions. Both must choose the same system (both state or both federal).

What happens to property that is not exempt?

In Chapter 7, the trustee may sell non-exempt property to pay creditors. In Chapter 13, non-exempt property value determines your minimum plan payment but you keep everything.

Is my retirement account protected?

Yes. 401(k)s, IRAs, and other qualified retirement accounts are fully exempt under both state and federal exemption systems.
