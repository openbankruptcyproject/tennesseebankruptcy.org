The Automatic Stay in Memphis Bankruptcy

The moment you file bankruptcy, the automatic stay stops most collection actions against you. This is one of the most powerful protections in bankruptcy law.

What the Automatic Stay Stops

Stopped by Automatic Stay

  • Foreclosure proceedings
  • Vehicle repossession
  • Wage garnishment
  • Lawsuits and judgments
  • Collection calls and letters
  • Utility disconnection (20 days)
  • Bank account levies

NOT Stopped

  • Criminal proceedings
  • Child support / alimony actions
  • Tax audits (assessment only)
  • Eviction (in some cases)
  • Pension loan repayment

Tennessee-Specific Concerns

Tennessee uses non-judicial foreclosure, meaning your lender can foreclose without going through court. This makes the automatic stay especially critical for Memphis homeowners -- it may be the only thing stopping a foreclosure sale.

However, be aware that repeat filers face reduced protection:

  • Second filing within 1 year: Automatic stay expires after 30 days unless you prove good faith
  • Third+ filing within 1 year: No automatic stay at all unless you get a court order

Given the 53.5% prior-filer rate in W.D. Tenn., many Memphis filers may face these limitations.

Learn more at automaticstay.org.
