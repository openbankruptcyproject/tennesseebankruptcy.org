Eliminating Medical Debt in Memphis Bankruptcy

Medical debt is the leading cause of bankruptcy in America. In Memphis, medical bills are fully dischargeable in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13.

Medical Debt in Bankruptcy

Medical debt is 100% dischargeable in bankruptcy. It is treated as general unsecured debt with no special protections for creditors.

Whether you owe $5,000 or $500,000 in medical bills, bankruptcy can eliminate it entirely. In Chapter 7, medical debt is wiped out in 3-4 months. In Chapter 13, you may pay a portion through your plan, with the remainder discharged.

Before Filing: Know Your Options

  • Negotiate directly: Many Memphis hospitals offer charity care or payment reductions
  • Check for billing errors: Medical billing errors are common
  • Statute of limitations: Tennessee's statute of limitations on medical debt is 6 years
  • Credit reporting: Medical debt under $500 no longer appears on credit reports

If negotiation is not enough and you have other debts too, bankruptcy may be the most effective solution.
