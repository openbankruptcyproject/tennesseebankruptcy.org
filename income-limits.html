Bankruptcy Income Limits in Tennessee

Your income determines which bankruptcy chapter you qualify for in Tennessee and how much you pay in Chapter 13. Here is how the income limits work.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Chapter 7 Income Limits

Chapter 7 uses the means test to determine eligibility. The test compares your household's current monthly income (averaged over the prior 6 months) to the Tennessee median income for your household size.

If Below Median Income

You automatically pass the means test and qualify for Chapter 7. No further calculation is needed.

If Above Median Income

You must complete the full means test, which deducts allowed expenses from your income. If the remaining disposable income falls below the threshold, you still qualify. The means test uses IRS expense standards plus your actual secured debt payments.

Tennessee median income figures are updated twice per year (April and October) by the U.S. Trustee Program. Check bankruptcymeanstest.org for current figures.

Chapter 13 -- No Income Ceiling

Chapter 13 has no maximum income limit. Anyone with regular income can file, regardless of how high that income is. However, your income affects:

  • Plan length: Above-median income requires a 5-year plan; below-median allows 3-5 years
  • Payment amount: Higher income means higher monthly plan payments
  • Debt limits: Total debts (secured plus unsecured) cannot exceed $2,750,000

Chapter 13 requires regular income -- wages, salary, self-employment, Social Security, pension, or other consistent sources.

Income Calculation Details

Current monthly income (CMI) includes almost all income from all sources over the 6 months before filing:

  • Wages, salary, overtime, bonuses, commissions
  • Self-employment income (gross receipts minus ordinary business expenses)
  • Interest, dividends, rental income
  • Pension, retirement, annuity payments
  • Unemployment compensation
  • Contributions from household members (spouse, non-filing partner)

Excluded from CMI: Social Security benefits, payments to victims of war crimes or terrorism.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the income limits for bankruptcy in Tennessee?

For Chapter 7, your household income must be at or below the Tennessee median for your household size, or you must pass the means test expense deduction. Chapter 13 has no income ceiling -- anyone with regular income can file. However, Chapter 13 has a debt limit of $2,750,000.

What if my income is too high for Chapter 7?

If your income exceeds the Tennessee median, you still might qualify through the means test expense deductions. If not, Chapter 13 allows any income level -- higher income just means higher plan payments over 3-5 years.

Does household size affect income limits?

Yes. The median income threshold increases with household size. A single filer has a lower threshold than a family of four. Household size typically includes everyone living in the home and dependents you support.
