Judgments and Bankruptcy in Tennessee

A civil judgment against you does not mean you are out of options. Bankruptcy can discharge the underlying debt and even remove judgment liens from your property in Tennessee.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Discharging Judgments in Bankruptcy

Whether a judgment is dischargeable depends on the type of debt behind it:

Generally Dischargeable

  • Credit card judgments
  • Medical debt judgments
  • Personal loan judgments
  • Breach of contract judgments
  • Deficiency judgments (auto, mortgage)

Generally NOT Dischargeable

  • Judgments based on fraud
  • Judgments for willful injury
  • DUI/DWI damage judgments
  • Domestic support judgments
  • Student loan judgments (absent hardship)
  • Government fine/penalty judgments

Removing Judgment Liens Under Section 522(f)

Even when a judgment is discharged, the lien it created on your property may survive unless you take action. Under 11 U.S.C. section 522(f), you can file a motion to avoid (remove) a judicial lien if it impairs your exemptions.

How It Works in Tennessee

  • Home: If your home equity minus the judgment lien and any mortgages is less than the $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint) homestead exemption, the lien impairs your exemption and can be avoided
  • Vehicle: Same analysis using the $10,000 vehicle exemption
  • Other property: Any exempt property with an impairing judgment lien qualifies
You must file a separate motion with the W.D. Tenn., M.D. Tenn., and E.D. Tenn. court to avoid the lien. The discharge alone does not remove it.

Stopping Judgment Enforcement

The moment you file bankruptcy, the automatic stay immediately stops all judgment enforcement:

  • Wage garnishment must stop
  • Bank account levies are frozen
  • Property liens cannot be enforced
  • No new collection lawsuits can be filed
  • Existing lawsuits are paused

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I discharge a judgment in bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes. Most money judgments based on unsecured debt (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) are dischargeable in bankruptcy in Tennessee. The underlying debt determines dischargeability -- if the original debt was dischargeable, the judgment based on that debt is also dischargeable.

Can I remove a judgment lien from my property?

Yes. Under 11 U.S.C. section 522(f), you can avoid (remove) a judicial lien on your property to the extent it impairs your bankruptcy exemptions. In Tennessee, this is commonly used to remove judgment liens from homes (homestead exemption: $5,000 (individual) / $7,500 (joint)) and vehicles (exemption: $10,000).

Does the judgment creditor have to stop collecting?

Yes. The automatic stay stops all collection on judgments the instant you file. The creditor cannot garnish wages, levy bank accounts, or enforce the judgment in any way while the stay is in effect.
