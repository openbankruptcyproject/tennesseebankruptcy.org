Can Bankruptcy Stop an Eviction in Tennessee?

Timing is everything. Bankruptcy can halt an eviction if you file before a judgment for possession is entered. After a judgment, the protection is limited.

Quick answer: If no eviction judgment has been entered, filing bankruptcy immediately stops the eviction through the automatic stay. If a judgment already exists, section 362(b)(22) allows the landlord to proceed. File before the judgment to maximize protection.

Before vs. After Eviction Judgment

Before Judgment

  • Automatic stay stops eviction
  • Landlord cannot proceed in state court
  • Chapter 13 can cure back rent over 3-5 years
  • You must pay post-petition rent going forward
  • Emergency filing possible in hours

After Judgment

  • Section 362(b)(22) limits protection
  • Landlord can proceed with eviction
  • Exception: if state law allows cure after judgment, you may file certification under 362(l)
  • Most Tennessee courts apply this strictly
  • Chapter 7 will discharge the rent debt but not stop eviction

Tennessee Eviction Timeline

Governing law: Tenn. Code 29-18-101 et seq.

Tennessee requires a 14-day notice for nonpayment (30 days for lease violations). Detainer warrant cases are heard in General Sessions Court, typically within 6-10 days of filing.

Understanding this timeline is critical for deciding when to file bankruptcy. Once the landlord obtains a judgment for possession, your bankruptcy protections are significantly reduced.

Key window: File bankruptcy BEFORE the judge enters a judgment for possession. Once a judgment is entered, section 362(b)(22) may allow the eviction to continue despite your filing.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 for Evictions

Chapter 7

  • Discharges back rent (pre-petition)
  • Automatic stay is temporary (case lasts 3-4 months)
  • Does not allow you to cure arrears
  • Best if you plan to move anyway
  • Eliminates personal liability for lease balance

Chapter 13

  • Allows curing back rent through plan
  • Automatic stay lasts 3-5 years (plan duration)
  • You must assume the lease and stay current
  • Best if you want to keep your housing
  • Plan payments include arrears catch-up

Emergency Filing to Stop Eviction

If eviction is imminent, an emergency bankruptcy filing can be done quickly:

  • Bare-bones petition: File just the petition, Statement of Social Security Number, and credit counseling certificate. You have 14 days to file the remaining schedules.
  • Immediate protection: The automatic stay takes effect the moment the petition is filed with the bankruptcy court -- not when the landlord is notified.
  • Proof of filing: Get a copy of the filed petition with the case number and give it to your landlord and the state court handling the eviction.
  • Filing fee: Chapter 7 costs $338 and Chapter 13 costs $313. Fee waivers are available for those below 150% of the poverty line.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy stop an eviction in Tennessee?

It depends on timing. If no eviction judgment has been entered, filing bankruptcy triggers the automatic stay, which immediately halts eviction proceedings. If a judgment for possession already exists, section 362(b)(22) limits the stay's protection, and the landlord may be able to proceed.

What is section 362(b)(22)?

Section 362(b)(22) of the Bankruptcy Code is an exception to the automatic stay. If a landlord obtained a judgment for possession before you filed bankruptcy, the stay does not prevent the landlord from continuing the eviction. This was added by the 2005 BAPCPA amendments to prevent abuse of bankruptcy to delay evictions.

What if I file bankruptcy before the eviction judgment in Tennessee?

If no judgment for possession has been entered, the automatic stay stops the eviction cold. The landlord cannot proceed with the eviction, change locks, or remove your property. However, you must still pay post-petition rent -- the stay does not excuse future rent payments.

Can I cure a lease default in Chapter 13?

In Chapter 13, you can cure lease defaults (back rent) through your repayment plan. You assume the lease and pay arrears over the plan period (3-5 years). This only works if your landlord has not yet obtained a judgment for possession.

Does filing bankruptcy erase back rent?

Chapter 7 can discharge back rent owed before filing, but it does not stop an eviction if a judgment already exists. Chapter 13 lets you cure arrears while keeping your home. Post-petition rent must always be paid regardless of the chapter filed.

How quickly can I file bankruptcy to stop an eviction in Tennessee?

Emergency (bare-bones) bankruptcy filings can be done in hours. You file the petition with minimal schedules and have 14 days to complete the rest. The automatic stay takes effect the moment the petition is filed with the court.
