Payday Loans and Bankruptcy in Tennessee

Payday loans trap borrowers in cycles of high-interest debt. Bankruptcy can break that cycle by discharging payday loan obligations entirely.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Payday Loans Are Dischargeable

Payday loans are unsecured consumer debt. They are fully dischargeable in bankruptcy -- meaning you will not owe anything after your case is completed.

  • Chapter 7: Payday loan debt is eliminated entirely in 3-4 months
  • Chapter 13: Payday loans are included in your repayment plan as general unsecured debt, typically receiving only a fraction of the balance

Stopping ACH Debits and Post-Dated Checks

Many payday lenders require ACH (electronic) authorization or post-dated checks as a condition of the loan. Once you file bankruptcy:

  • The automatic stay prohibits the lender from processing any further debits
  • Revoke your ACH authorization in writing with both the lender and your bank
  • Notify your bank of the bankruptcy filing and request a stop payment on any post-dated checks
  • If the lender processes a payment after filing, they have violated the stay and may owe you damages
Take action immediately upon filing: contact your bank, revoke ACH authorizations in writing, and keep copies of all communications.

Can Payday Lenders Challenge Discharge?

In rare cases, a payday lender might argue that the loan was obtained through fraud (11 U.S.C. section 523(a)(2)). To succeed, they would need to prove:

  • You made a false representation (e.g., lied about income or employment)
  • You intended to deceive the lender
  • The lender relied on your misrepresentation

In practice, payday lenders rarely challenge discharge because the amounts are small and the legal costs are high. Taking out a payday loan when you know you plan to file bankruptcy could raise issues, so consult an attorney about timing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I discharge payday loans in bankruptcy in Tennessee?

Yes. Payday loans are unsecured debt and are fully dischargeable in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy in Tennessee. Once discharged, the lender cannot collect, even if they have post-dated checks or ACH authorization.

Can a payday lender cash my check after I file bankruptcy?

No. The automatic stay prevents payday lenders from cashing post-dated checks or processing ACH debits after you file. If they do, it is a stay violation and you may be entitled to damages.

Should I stop paying payday loans before filing?

Once you decide to file bankruptcy, continuing to pay payday loans means less money available for necessary living expenses. However, consult an attorney about timing, especially regarding any pending ACH authorizations or post-dated checks.
