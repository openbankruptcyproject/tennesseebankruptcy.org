Tennessee Non-Judicial Foreclosure

Tennessee is a non-judicial foreclosure state. Chapter 13's automatic stay is the most effective tool to stop foreclosure in Knoxville.

How Chapter 13 Works for Knoxville Filers

Chapter 13 is a court-supervised repayment plan, typically running three to five years. It is the right tool when you have regular income, want to catch up on a mortgage arrearage, protect a non-exempt asset, or failed the Chapter 7 means test. In Knoxville, Chapter 13 plans are administered by the standing trustee for your division, who collects your monthly payment and distributes it to creditors according to the confirmed plan.

The Chapter 13 process starts the same way as Chapter 7: credit counseling, petition, schedules, and the automatic stay. The key differences begin at filing. Along with your schedules, you file a proposed plan showing how much you will pay the trustee each month, for how many months, and how that money will be distributed. Your first plan payment is due within 30 days of filing - even before the plan is confirmed by the judge - and missing it is the single most common reason Chapter 13 cases are dismissed early.

Your plan payment is calculated by taking your monthly disposable income (from Form 122C) and applying a few rules. Above-median debtors must commit all disposable income for 60 months. Below-median debtors must commit all disposable income for 36 months. Secured debts being cured in the plan - a mortgage arrearage, a car loan being crammed down - are added on top. Priority debts like recent taxes and child support arrearage must be paid in full through the plan. General unsecured creditors get whatever is left after all of that.

When Chapter 13 Makes Sense

  • Mortgage arrearage: Chapter 13 lets you cure a foreclosure arrearage over up to 60 months while making current payments outside the plan.
  • Underwater car loans over 910 days old: You can "cram down" the loan to the car's actual value and pay the rest as unsecured.
  • Non-dischargeable tax debt: Priority taxes can be paid over 60 months with the automatic stay in place, stopping IRS collection.
  • Above-median income: If you failed the Chapter 7 means test, Chapter 13 is the fallback and always available.
  • Keeping non-exempt assets: If you have equity above your exemption limits, Chapter 13 lets you keep the asset in exchange for paying the equity value to unsecured creditors through the plan.

About half of all Chapter 13 cases nationally fail to reach discharge. The most common reasons are missed plan payments and post-filing income disruptions. Before filing, make sure the plan payment is actually affordable given your real monthly budget - not an optimistic budget built to squeeze past the confirmation hearing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a Knoxville Chapter 13 plan last?

Three years if you are below the state median income, five years if you are above. The judge cannot confirm a plan shorter than three years unless it pays 100 percent of unsecured claims.

Can I pay off my Knoxville Chapter 13 plan early?

Only by paying 100 percent of unsecured claims. If you try to pay off a plan early with less than 100 percent to unsecureds, the trustee and unsecured creditors can object - and courts generally sustain those objections because the Bankruptcy Code requires committing all projected disposable income for the full commitment period.

What happens if I miss a Chapter 13 payment in Knoxville?

The trustee will file a motion to dismiss after one or two missed payments. You have a chance to cure the default by catching up, modifying the plan to add the missed amount to future payments, or converting to Chapter 7. Do not ignore a motion to dismiss - dismissal without a discharge means all your creditors come back.
