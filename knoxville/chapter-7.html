Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Knoxville

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts in 3-4 months. Here is what Knoxville residents need to know.

What Is Chapter 7?

Chapter 7 eliminates credit cards, medical bills, and personal loans. Most Knoxville filers keep all property within exemption limits.

Your Chapter 7 Case in Knoxville -- What to Expect

Chapter 7 is the fastest path out of unmanageable debt. In Knoxville, most Chapter 7 cases close 90 to 120 days from the filing date, and the vast majority are 'no asset' cases where the debtor keeps everything they own. The automatic stay starts the moment you file, which means creditors cannot call, sue, garnish, or repossess while your case is pending. Here is what the process actually looks like for Knoxville filers.

Before you file, you complete a pre-filing credit counseling course from an approved provider -- usually online, about 60 minutes, under $25. You also gather your documents: the last two years of tax returns, the last six months of pay stubs, three months of bank statements, titles to your vehicles, your mortgage statement if you own a home, and a list of every creditor. Your petition, schedules, and statement of financial affairs are then filed electronically with the bankruptcy court. Filing opens a new case number and triggers the automatic stay.

About 20 to 40 days after filing, you attend the section 341 meeting of creditors. In Tennessee, most 341 meetings are now conducted by Zoom or phone rather than in person. You are placed under oath and questioned by the Chapter 7 trustee about your schedules. Typical questions: Did you list all your assets? Have you transferred anything valuable in the last two years? Do you expect a tax refund or inheritance? The meeting usually lasts five to ten minutes. Creditors rarely attend.

After the 341, the trustee investigates whether any of your assets can be sold for the benefit of creditors. In the great majority of Knoxville Chapter 7 cases, the answer is no -- your exempt property is protected, and the trustee files a "no distribution" report. You complete a second class (a post-filing financial management course) and wait. Roughly 60 days after the 341 meeting, the court issues your discharge order, wiping out the dischargeable debts and giving you a fresh start.

What Chapter 7 Does Not Wipe Out

  • Student loans in most cases (requires adversary proceeding for hardship discharge)
  • Recent tax debts (generally anything less than three years old)
  • Child support and alimony
  • Criminal fines and restitution
  • Debts obtained by fraud or false pretenses
  • Debts not listed on your schedules

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a Knoxville Chapter 7 take from filing to discharge?

Most cases discharge 90 to 120 days after filing. The minimum statutory period is 60 days after the 341 meeting. Cases with asset sales or objections take longer -- sometimes a year or more.

Can I keep my car in a Knoxville Chapter 7 case?

Usually yes, if you are current on the loan and your equity is within your exemption limits. You can either 'reaffirm' the debt (sign a new agreement to keep paying), 'redeem' it (pay the current value in one lump sum), or let it go. Most filers reaffirm.

How much does a Chapter 7 attorney cost in Knoxville?

Attorney fees vary, but the typical range for a straightforward consumer Chapter 7 in most districts runs from about $1,000 to $2,500 plus the $338 court filing fee. Be careful with firms advertising '$0 down' Chapter 7 -- upfront attorney fees in Chapter 7 are discharged if unpaid at filing, so 'no money down' is usually a Chapter 13 pitch.
